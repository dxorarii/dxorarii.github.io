Category #1: Food

Louisiana Creole cuisine is a style of cooking that blends West African, French, Spanish, and Native American influences, as well as influences from the general cuisine of the Southern United States. Louisiana is known for its Cajun and Creole cuisine, which are relatively similar to each other. Popular Louisiana foods include gumbo, étouffée, jambalaya, ya-ka-mein, and grillades and grits. Famous Louisiana foods include gumbo, beignets, pecan pralines, and seafood and sausage gumbo.

Category #2: Tradition

Foodways such as gumbo, jambalaya, crawfish etouffee, and crawfish boils Music such as Cajun music and zydeco, and jazz funerals. Material culture such as Creole cottages, shotgun houses, pirogues, and bateaux Ritual/festive practices such as folk Catholicism, home altars, traiteurs, and Mardi Gras Languages such as Cajun and Creole French, Spanish, Dalmatian, Indian languages Architecture, Art, literature, games, and sports.

Category #3: Clothes

Incorporate Bold Colors and Prints Louisiana is known for its vibrant and colorful fashion. Bright and bold colors are a must-have in your wardrobe. Add a Touch of Cajun Flair Cajun culture is an integral part of Louisiana’s heritage. You can add a touch to your wardrobe with accessories like beads, feathers, and hats. Embrace Southern Style The southern style heavily influences Louisiana’s fashion.
